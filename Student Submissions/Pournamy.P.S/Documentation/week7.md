This week focused on dashboard creation and effective data presentation techniques using Power BI. The primary objective was to understand how to represent both categorical and numerical (integer) data in a clear, structured, and meaningful manner.
The process began with designing the overall dashboard layout. A proper title was created using the Text Box feature in Power BI, with emphasis on making it clear, unique, and relevant to the dashboard’s purpose.
Key Performance Indicator (KPI) cards were then introduced to highlight the most important metrics. Only essential measures were included to ensure clarity and avoid unnecessary clutter.
Various visualization techniques were explored, including bar charts, pie charts, and slicers. Each chart type was used based on the nature of the data for instance, bar charts for comparisons and pie charts for proportion-based insights. The concept of legends was also discussed, which helps in identifying different categories through colors or labels. It was noted that pie charts should ideally contain no more than five to six categories to maintain readability.

For numerical or integer-based data, the dashboard was structured by dividing it into clear sections or frames. This approach improves organization and enhances user understanding.
The concept of proper framing was also introduced. Framing refers to the systematic arrangement of visuals within a dashboard to ensure logical flow, readability, and ease of navigation.
Additionally, the importance of pre-configuring and planning the dashboard layout before final development was emphasized, as it helps in creating a clean, efficient, and well-organized final output.
Overall, this week provided a strong foundation in designing structured and visually effective dashboards.
