This week focused on gaining a deeper understanding of Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA). We began by learning how to understand a dataset by examining its structure, including the number of rows and columns, and identifying the types of data present. We also discussed different types of datasets, such as structured and unstructured data.

We then covered key preprocessing steps, starting with handling missing values to ensure data quality, followed by handling outliers, which are extreme values that lie outside the normal range and can affect analysis results. Additionally, we learned about data categorization in Power BI, where data is classified into numeric (numbers), categorical (labels or text), and time-series (data over time) types.

Overall, the focus was on three main steps: understanding the dataset, handling missing values, and handling outliers to prepare the data effectively for analysis.
